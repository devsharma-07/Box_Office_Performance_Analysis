### Project Overview:

I analyzed a dataset of Marvel Cinematic Universe movies (2008–2021) to uncover what drives box office performance, a question central to content investment decisions at studios, streaming platforms, and entertainment analytics teams. To achieve this, I examined relationships between production budgets, audience scores, critic ratings, and revenue across all four MCU phases.

Next, I built a supervised linear regression model to predict worldwide box office collections from opening weekend numbers, revealing that the model overpredicted 55.6% of the time with an average error of 15.17%. These findings highlight the limits of single-variable revenue forecasting and the need for richer feature sets in entertainment prediction models. Additional analysis found a correlation of 0.795 between production budget and worldwide revenue, suggesting that studio investment decisions are a meaningful, but imperfect, signal of commercial success.

**Marvel Box Office Dataset: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/davidgdong/marvel-cinematic-universe-box-office-dataset**
